# DSA Roadmap

## Phases

| Phase       | Focus         | Daily Questions      |                    Goal                                        |
|-------------|---------------|----------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Phase 1** | Easy + Medium | 1 Easy + 1 Medium    | Build problem-solving habits and recognize common patterns.    |
| **Phase 2** | Medium        | 2 Medium             | Solve most Medium problems without looking at hints.           |
| **Phase 3** | Medium + Hard | 1 Medium + 1 Hard    | Learn optimization techniques and advanced data structures.    |
| **Phase 4** | Hard          | 2 Hard               | Combine multiple concepts naturally to solve complex problems. |
| **Phase 5** | Very Hard     | 1 Hard + 1 Very Hard | Think like a senior engineer solving real-world algorithmic and system-level problems.                                                                                              |

---

## My Definition of "Solved"

A problem is **not** solved just because it passes all test cases.

A problem is considered **solved** only if I can:

- ✅ Explain the **brute-force** solution.
- ✅ Explain **why the brute-force approach is inefficient**.
- ✅ Derive the **optimal solution** step by step.
- ✅ Code the solution **from scratch** without assistance.
- ✅ Analyze the **time and space complexity**.
- ✅ Explain the **underlying algorithmic pattern**.
- ✅ Recognize and solve **similar problems** in the future.

> **Rule:** If I cannot confidently do **all** of the above, the problem goes back into the **revision queue**.

---

## Philosophy

> **Passing the test cases is the beginning—not the finish line.**
>
> True mastery comes from understanding *why* the solution works, *when* to apply it, and *how* to derive it independently.
